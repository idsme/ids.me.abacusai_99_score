## So what does the next level look like?

Agents that own entire workflows. Specifications precise enough that machines execute them without hand-holding. Teams that stop managing AI tools and start managing AI outcomes. The shift from AI-as-assistant to AI-as-infrastructure. Most organizations aren't having that conversation yet. I've been living in it.

## I started at CMG Advanced Technology Division

Pure R&D, before any of this was commercial. I built tracking and tracing systems and 2D barcode solutions before Google Maps went online. I worked on cell broadcasting systems that would later underpin things like Apple's Find My iPhone. I wrote one of the first smartphone apps on a Nokia, before 2001. That early love for R&D never left — what changed is where I point it. For the past 25 years, the pattern has been the same: find the gap between what technology makes possible and what businesses and their engineering teams are actually doing with it. Then close it.

## That's meant coaching over a hundred engineers

Building training programs from zero. Walking into enterprise teams buried under 20+ person-years of legacy and getting them to ship again — and helping them find their fire again. The technology is only ever half the problem. Getting smart people to move is the other half.
